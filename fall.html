Low-Prep, High-Flavour Porcini Risotto

The slow stirring of risotto is what makes a lot of people think the dish is complicated, or lengthy, to make. But the cooking, just a method of slowly
adding liquid to rice as it simmers, only takes 18 minutes. It’s the prep and ingredients that’ll slow you down.

The hurdle is the expectation of using homemade chicken stock. But if you’re bumping it up with other strong flavour, you can sidestep this: don't make stock,
don't chop vegetables, a fistful of dried porcini mushrooms will give Arborio rice all the mushroom flavour you need.

Ingredients

  1. 2 loose cups dried porcini mushrooms (often sold in 1 oz. packages that are about 1 cup each)
  2. 6 cups chicken or vegetable stock, hot
  3. 3 tbsp butter, unsalted
  4. 1 garlic clove, minced
  5. 2 cups Arborio rice
  6. 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  7. Salt and pepper to taste
